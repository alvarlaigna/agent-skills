# Authoritative Estonian Sources

Use this reference when you need source-backed confidence for Estonian spelling, forms, orthography, or language advice.

## Source Priority

1. **Sõnaveeb / ÕS**: word entries, accepted spelling, forms, meanings, usage labels, and written-standard guidance.
2. **EKI teatmik**: orthography, punctuation, capitalization, abbreviations, names, numbers, and language advice articles.
3. **EKI search**: broad search across EKI materials when you do not know which article or dictionary entry applies.
4. **estonian-mcp**: fast automated checks and morphology workflows when the connector is installed.

Prefer source-backed answers over memory. If you cannot verify a claim, label it as a style judgment or uncertainty.

## Sõnaveeb / ÕS

Use Sõnaveeb/ÕS for:

- spelling and accepted variants,
- declension/conjugation forms,
- meanings and usage labels,
- compound or separate-writing doubts,
- whether a word belongs to neutral standard Estonian.

Relevant entry point: https://sonaveeb.ee/os

Notes:

- ÕS 2025 is the basis of the written standard from 2026.
- Keep Estonian characters literal: `õ`, `ä`, `ö`, `ü`, `š`, `ž`.
- If Sõnaveeb shows several variants, preserve the user's intended register and pick the safest standard option unless the task calls for colloquial language.

## EKI Teatmik

Use EKI teatmik for:

- capitalization and initial-letter rules,
- punctuation,
- abbreviation endings and hyphenation,
- number writing,
- foreign names and place names,
- wording advice and language-planning recommendations.

Relevant entry point: https://eki.ee/teatmik/

When citing or relying on an article, use the article's exact rule wording where possible. Do not over-generalize a narrow advice article into a universal ban.

## EKI Main Site

Use https://eki.ee/ when:

- the user asks for official language resources,
- you need a broader EKI search path,
- Sõnaveeb and EKI teatmik are not enough to locate the relevant material.

## Practical Lookup Pattern

For a disputed word or phrase:

1. Search Sõnaveeb/ÕS for the word or lemma.
2. If the issue is punctuation, capitalization, abbreviation, number formatting, or a style recommendation, search EKI teatmik.
3. If the phrase may be a calque or coined compound, verify the parts and search for a more idiomatic Estonian expression.
4. Report the result as:
   - `Correction`: source-backed change.
   - `Suggestion`: style or naturalness improvement.
   - `Uncertain`: not confidently resolved from available sources.

## Do Not

- Do not present memory-only inflections as authoritative.
- Do not treat every dictionary absence as proof that a word is impossible.
- Do not replace domain terminology just because it feels foreign; check whether it is established Estonian usage.
- Do not cite a tool or source inside polished copy meant for an end audience.
